Apartheid's Two-Phase Development

Phase 1: Foundation (1948-1953)

  • Population Registration: Who you are
  • Group Areas: Where you live
  • Bantu Education: What you learn
  • Focus: Major legal framework
Result: Legal apartheid established

Phase 2: Expansion (1954-1959)

  • Separate Amenities: Every public space
  • University Education: Higher education control
  • Bantu Self-Government: Political channeling
  • Focus: Total system completion
Result: No aspect of life uncontrolled

The Three New Laws (1954-1959)

1953

Separate Amenities Act

"Petty Apartheid"

  • Parks, beaches, buses segregated
  • Toilets, benches, cinemas separated
  • Made apartheid visible daily
  • No "equal" requirement
1959

Extension of University Education Act

"Higher Education Apartheid"

  • Separate universities for each race
  • Closed "open" universities
  • Controlled professional training
  • Limited black advancement
1959

Bantu Self-Government Act

"Political Apartheid"

  • African "homelands" system
  • Tribal authorities empowered
  • Separate political development
  • Removed from "white" politics

Two Types of Control

Grand Apartheid

Major structural separations

  • Group Areas Act (where you live)
  • Bantu Education (what you learn)
  • University Education (higher learning)
  • Bantu Self-Government (political rights)
Purpose: Separate the races completely

Petty Apartheid

Daily life segregations

  • Separate Amenities (parks, benches, toilets)
  • Transport segregation (buses, trains)
  • Employment restrictions (job types)
  • Social interaction controls (mixing prohibited)
Purpose: Make inequality visible and humiliating
